Hi! I'm Na Hyung.

... It rhymes with "Ma-Young," a made-up word.

Welcome! I'm a Seattle-area software engineer specializing in JavaScript and Python. I currently work at Hazel Analytics, doing full-stack web development for our main product, Food Safety Insights. Prior to that, I worked as a frontend engineer at the Allen Institute for Cell Science, where I helped build open-source cell science applications such as Simularium and the napari plugin for the Allen Cell & Structure Segmenter.

I have a background in geology. As a student, I romped around landscapes all over the United States mapping rock outcrops, studying soil profiles, and researching active faults. After graduation, I sharpened my GIS and data visualization skills as a Research Fellow at the National Energy Technology Lab. Then, I spent 2.5 years as a consulting geologist, assessing and mitigating geologic hazards such as landslides and liquefaction in the Pacific Northwest and developing valuable customer-facing communication and coordination skills.

While working as a geologist, I thought of some mobile apps that I wished existed. I thought maybe I could build them myself someday. So I started teaching myself to code on Codecademy in my free time. This new hobby reminded me of how much I enjoyed learning and using MATLAB for my graduate research. After I learned about coding boot camps, I decided to pursue a new career in software. Then I took the next step forward by completing a web development program at Epicodus in 2019.

I thrive when I'm constantly learning new things. I believe that the quality of code and internal documentation lend directly to the quality of user experience. I love that I get to collaborate with hard-working, talented people every day at work.

Outside of work, I'm happiest when I'm in exhausting myself the mountains or admiring rock outcrops in the desert. I also love to cook, take photos, make music, and geek out with fountain pens.
